## Why

The current Talent Pool content describes a generic elite development program, but the federation's actual role is more specific: scout promising players in Belgium, support their long-term development alongside their clubs, and gradually prepare them for future national-team environments. This should be clarified now so the public pathway, age-category pages, and future editorial work all reflect the real purpose of the Talent Pool.

## What Changes

- Reframe the public Talent Pool narrative around federation scouting, long-term preparation, and support for players developing primarily within their clubs.
- Define clear age-stage purposes for U14, U15, and U16 so each category explains a distinct role in the pathway rather than presenting the same elite-program message at increasing intensity.
- Introduce a clearer public distinction between club development, Talent Pool preparation, and national-team selection.
- Establish a page structure for the Talent Pool landing page and category pages that explains purpose, pathway progression, international exposure, and future national-team connection.
- Create a content model direction for stage-specific narrative content so future updates are not trapped in generic hardcoded copy.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `talent-pool-pathway`: Define the federation-facing Talent Pool pathway, including its public positioning, stage progression, and relationship to clubs and national teams.

### Modified Capabilities
- None.

## Impact

- Affected code surfaces include the public Talent Pool landing page, homepage Talent Pool summaries, category detail pages, category skills pages, navigation copy, and any supporting Sanity-backed content used for Talent Pool messaging.
- Affected editorial model includes Talent Pool category descriptions and any future structured fields needed to express age-stage purpose, pathway expectations, and federation concepts.
- No external APIs or dependencies are required for this change definition.
