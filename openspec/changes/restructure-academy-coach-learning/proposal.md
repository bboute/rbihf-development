# Proposal: Restructure Academy Coach Learning

## Summary
Reframe RBIHF Academy from a broad future learning hub into a coach-first education surface. The Academy should support official in-person coaching courses, explain progression through qualification pathways, and offer extra learning content such as clinics, explanatory videos, and topic-based coaching guidance.

## Problem
The current Academy page is a generic placeholder aimed at players, parents, coaches, and staff all at once. That makes the product vague and hides the strongest real use case: coach development. At the same time, current course delivery is still primarily in-person, manual-based, and teacher-led over weekend sessions, so the website should not pretend to be a full online course platform yet.

## Goals
- Make Academy clearly coach-first.
- Separate official qualification pathways from extra learning content.
- Support current in-person course delivery without forcing a full LMS model.
- Create room for future RBIHF-owned trainer and coach pathways.
- Make practical coaching help easy to browse by topic.

## Non-Goals
- Replace in-person weekend courses with full digital course delivery.
- Build quizzes, certification tracking, or LMS-style progress features.
- Solve all future taxonomy and multilingual needs in one step.
- Redesign Club Development within this change.

## Proposed Outcome
The Academy should become the federation’s coach education platform with two main branches:
1. Qualification Pathways
2. Coach Learning Library

Qualification Pathways should present current official tracks such as Learn to Play, Level 1, and Level 2, including who they are for, how they are delivered, and what comes next.

The Coach Learning Library should contain topic-based content such as:
- running a practice with kids
- practice design
- body checking clinics
- systems teaching
- explanatory videos
- age-specific coaching guidance

## Why Now
RBIHF already has real coach education activity, but the site does not reflect it clearly. Reframing Academy now creates a realistic digital role for the website while leaving space to grow into richer learning products later.
