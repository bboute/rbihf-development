# Proposal: Add Club Development Pillar

## Summary
Add a fourth public pillar to the RBIHF website called Club Development. This pillar will unify the current fragmented concepts of fundamentals, curriculum, club growth, and club discovery into one clear federation pathway for youth development across Belgium.

## Problem
The public website currently presents Academy, Talent Pool, National Teams, and Find a Club as peer-level destinations. The fourth slot is a utility rather than a true program pillar, while the actual youth development concept is split across separate surfaces:
- public fundamentals content
- portal curriculum content
- club growth content for staff
- club finder for families

This makes the product model harder to understand and weakens the federation message that every club should deliver the basics well.

## Goals
- Establish Club Development as the fourth top-level pillar.
- Present a clear age-based development pathway for youth players.
- Define minimum standards for practice setup, staffing, and delivery quality.
- Make the framework understandable to coaches, club leaders, parents, and helpers.
- Reposition Find a Club as a supporting action within the broader development model.

## Non-Goals
- Rebuild the full curriculum authoring system in one change.
- Create a certification or compliance workflow for clubs.
- Replace Talent Pool or National Teams pathway content.
- Solve multilingual content management in the same change.

## Proposed Outcome
The website should communicate a coherent four-pillar model:
1. Academy
2. Talent Pool
3. National Teams
4. Club Development

Club Development should act as the public umbrella for:
- age-band pathway guidance
- practice and staffing standards
- role-specific guidance for parents, coaches, helpers, and club leaders
- deeper curriculum and club-support resources in the portal

## Why Now
The repo already contains early versions of the required concepts, but they are disconnected. Formalizing this as a pillar lets RBIHF communicate a federation-wide youth development standard instead of isolated pages and tools.
